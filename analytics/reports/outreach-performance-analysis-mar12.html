Why Replies Are Near Zero — Root Cause Analysis

1. InMail Blind Spot Is Now CLOSED — And the News Is Bad
Sales Navigator inbox was fully audited on Mar 12. Out of 227 InMails sent: 0 accepted, 2 declined (Abe Blanco, Lance Silverman), ~137 still "Awaiting Response." The hope that InMail replies were hiding unread was wrong. The 0.9% InMail response rate (2 declines out of 227) is dramatically below LinkedIn's published 10-25% InMail response benchmark.
2. Most Emails Are Still in Touch 1 (Too Early for Replies)
The TAM Outbound campaign (111 emails, 22.5% of sends) only started Mar 10. As of Mar 12, most prospects have had the email for 24-48 hours. Industry data shows that 80% of cold email replies come 2-7 days after send. Waves 1-2 T2 follow-ups aren't due until Mar 15. It's simply too early to judge TAM Outbound reply performance.
3. Website Visitor Emails Have Low Open Rates (13.3%)
128 delivered with only 17 opens suggests possible deliverability issues, spam filtering, or subject line problems. The .net domain issue (22 T2 emails sent from robert.gorham@testsigma.net instead of .com) could be hurting sender reputation. Website visitor intent signal may also be weaker than expected.
4. No Multi-Touch Sequence Has Completed
Most prospects have only received Touch 1. BDR reply rates typically spike at Touch 2-3 (the follow-up), not Touch 1. The campaign cadence has been building pipeline but hasn't had time to execute the full sequence for most contacts.
5. High Bounce Rate Signals Data Quality Issues
11% bounce rate on TAM Outbound (12/109) is well above the 2-3% industry acceptable threshold. Humana (100%), Commvault (40%), and Mastercard (25%) bounces suggest Apollo's enrichment data for large enterprise contacts may be stale. Every bounce hurts sender reputation and future deliverability.
6. Domain Confusion May Affect Trust
22 emails were sent from testsigma.net instead of testsigma.com (B9/B10 T2 batch on Mar 9). If prospects Google "testsigma.net" and find nothing, it erodes trust. This was caught and corrected for B11+, but those 22 contacts received emails from an unfamiliar domain.
