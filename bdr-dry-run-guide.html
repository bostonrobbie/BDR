What Is This Pipeline?
▼

The BDR Automation Pipeline is a 12-step end-to-end system for prospecting QA leaders at mid-to-large companies. It combines:

  • Sales Navigator for prospect sourcing and InMail sending
  • Apollo for contact enrichment and sequence enrollment
  • Gmail & Google Calendar for follow-up email and meeting scheduling
  • Data-driven messaging based on 1,326 LinkedIn conversations analyzed
  • 3-touch multi-channel sequences across LinkedIn and email

Each batch consists of 25 carefully researched prospects with personalized, 3-touch sequences. The pipeline ensures consistency, quality, and compounding results.

What Is A/B Testing Here?
▼

Split each 25-prospect batch into 2-3 sub-groups. Test ONE variable at a time across groups. Variables to rotate:

  • Pain hook: Group A gets "maintenance/flaky tests", Group B gets "release velocity/speed"
  • Proof point style: Group A gets named customer, Group B gets anonymous
  • Opener style: Group A gets career-reference openers, Group B gets company-metric openers
  • Ask intensity: Group A gets "Would 15 minutes make sense?", Group B gets "Happy to share more"
  • Message length: Group A gets 70-80 words, Group B gets 100-120 words

Only test ONE variable per batch. Keep everything else constant. Track results and compare across 3+ batches before drawing conclusions.

ℹ️
No previous batches yet? That's fine. Use default settings: target Architects and Manager/Lead level, use Hansard/CRED/Sanofi proof points, send at lunch 12-1 PM, use "what day works" CTA. Define your A/B test variable and move forward.

5 Close Patterns (Rotate These)
▼

Use different close structures for different prospects. Rotate patterns across the batch:

  1. "If [outcome] would help [their situation], what day works for a quick look at how they did it?"
    Example: "If 3 fewer weeks of regression would help the OpenEdge schedule, what day works for a quick look at how they did it?"
  2. "What day works to see how [customer] [achieved outcome] while [solving their version of the problem]?"
    Example: "What day works to see how Medibuddy cut test upkeep in half while keeping coverage ahead of releases?"
  3. "If [outcome] before [their event/deadline] sounds useful, what day works for a quick look?"
    Example: "If 70% less Selenium maintenance before the refresh sounds useful, what day works for a quick look?"
  4. "What day works to compare how [customer] [achieved outcome] without [their constraint]?"
    Example: "What day works to compare how CRED hit 90% regression without hiring more QA?"
  5. "If getting [specific number] back in your [their process] would help, what day works for a quick chat?"
    Example: "If getting 3 fewer weeks back in your release cycle would help, what day works for a quick chat?"

Rule: No two prospects in the same batch should have structurally identical closes. Rotate patterns to add variety.

C2 Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)
▼

The "Stitched Template" Problem: Messages that look like 3 AI blocks glued together. C2 fixes this. Detect and rewrite if you see:

  • Opener contains 2+ company facts that don't connect to each other
  • Context sentence uses "That's the [noun] most [type] teams hit/feel/face" (formulaic)
  • Proof point could be swapped into any message and still make sense (not tied to theme)
  • Close is generic ("Would exploring that be worth your time?" works for ANY message)
  • Close doesn't reference the proof point's specific outcome
  • Close doesn't connect to the prospect's specific situation
  • Close uses open-ended question instead of "what day works" (14.0% vs 40.4% reply rate)
  • Message contains 2+ hyphens used as mid-sentence dashes (AI signature)
  • Message has no paragraph breaks between sections (wall of text on mobile)

If ANY of these appear → REWRITE.

⚠️
Common drafting mistakes: Using "I noticed" (-13.4 pp). Leading with AI/self-healing feature (-9.2 pp). Over 120 words (every word costs you). 3+ question marks instead of 2 (14.3% vs 34.8%). Using bullet lists (-2.2 pp). Generic closes that fit any message. Hyphens as mid-sentence dashes (AI signature). Fix these first before QA Gate.

Message Quality Score (MQS) - 12-Point System
▼

Every message scored 1-3 on 4 dimensions. Max = 12. Need ≥9/12 to send.

Dimension 1: Opener Clarity (1-3)

  • 3 = Specific, insight-driven question about prospect's situation. No "I noticed."
  • 2 = Company-specific reference but generic frame
  • 1 = "I noticed", role-recap, "Are you looking for..." (template-visible)

Dimension 2: CTA Confidence (1-3)

  • 3 = "What day works" with direct tie to proof outcome AND prospect situation. Can't be pasted elsewhere.
  • 2 = Uses "what day works" but tie to proof point is weak
  • 1 = "Would it be unreasonable", "happy to share if helpful", open-ended question close

Dimension 3: Personalization Density (1-3)

  • 3 = References something only THIS person would recognize (project, career move, company event)
  • 2 = References company specifically but could apply to anyone in that role
  • 1 = Only swaps name/title/company (indistinguishable from template)

Dimension 4: Friction (1-3, inverted: 3=low friction)

  • 3 = 80-110 words, one proof point, one CTA, 2 questions, 4+ breaks, max 1 hyphen, scannable on mobile
  • 2 = 110-120 words, may have 2 proof points, readable, proper spacing
  • 1 = Over 120 words, bullet lists, multiple CTAs, 3+ questions, wall-of-text, 2+ hyphens

Scoring: 10-12 = Ready to send. 7-9 = Acceptable but improve. <7 = MUST rewrite.

Step 10: Reply Tracking & Warm Lead Response

Reply Tag Definitions
▼

What triggered each reply? Use these tags to understand what resonates:

  • Opener: They referenced or reacted to the personalized opener question
  • Pain hook: They said "yeah, that's exactly our issue" or engaged with the problem
  • Proof point: They asked about the customer story or numbers
  • Timing: They said "good timing" or "we're evaluating now"
  • Referral: They forwarded to someone else or said "talk to [name]"
  • Not interested: Replied but declined (still valuable data)
  • Unknown: Can't tell what triggered it

Even negative replies are data. "Not interested" tells you the pain hook missed. "Talk to [name]" tells you we hit the right company but wrong person.
