# Gold Standard Messages

These are reference examples for each touch type. They represent the target quality, tone, and structure that every drafted message should match. Use these as calibration, not templates. Never copy structure verbatim.

---

## Why These Were Selected

Gold standards are pulled from the highest-MQS-scoring messages across batches and from patterns that correlate with replies in the 1,330-conversation dataset. Each example is annotated with what makes it work.

## Touch 1 - InMail (C1 Full Message)

### Example A: QA Director at FinServ Company
**MQS: 11/12 | Personalization Score: 3 | 87 words**

> **Subject:** Regression testing at OppFi
>
> Hi Dawn,
>
> With OppFi's AI evaluating 500+ attributes per application, and compliance rules shifting state by state, how is your team keeping regression coverage tight without slowing releases?
>
> That's the thing that usually compounds for fintech QA teams, every new state or model update means another round of full regression.
>
> Sanofi used Testsigma to cut their regression from 3 days to 80 minutes while keeping full traceability.
>
> Would 15 minutes to compare approaches make sense for what your team's dealing with?
>
> Cheers,
> Rob

**Why it works:**
- Opens with a specific, insight-driven question about OppFi's actual product (500+ attributes, state-by-state compliance)
- Context sentence explains WHY this matters for fintech QA teams specifically
- Proof point matches vertical (pharma/compliance translates to fintech compliance)
- CTA directly references "compare approaches" tied to the regression theme
- 87 words. Clean, scannable.
- No "I noticed," no role-recap, no feature headline

Pain: "I'm curious if managing integrations across practice management systems is making regression testing a headache."

```

### Example: VP Engineering at SaaS
```
Opener: "Hey Dan, this is Rob from Testsigma - we help engineering teams automate testing without the scripting overhead."
Pain: "With G2 processing millions of reviews in real-time, I imagine the release velocity pressure is pretty intense."
Bridge: "CRED hit 90% regression automation and 5x faster execution. Worth a quick minute?"

**Rules reminder:**
- 3 lines max
- Different pain hypothesis than InMail touches
- Different proof point than InMail touches
- Specific enough it doesn't sound scripted

## Anti-Patterns (what to avoid)

### The Template Reveal
> "Hey [Name], I saw you worked at [Company] for [X] years leading [team]. That must mean [assumption]. [Client] saved [number]. Would 15 minutes make sense?"

Every prospect has seen this 100 times. Dead on arrival.

### The Feature Dump
> "Testsigma offers AI-powered test automation with self-healing tests, NLP test creation, cross-browser testing, mobile testing, API testing, and Salesforce testing."

Zero high-performers use feature lists. Frame everything as outcomes.

### The Resume Recap
> "With your 8 years leading QA at Acme Corp and your background in Selenium automation..."

Never reference LinkedIn research directly. Synthesize, don't cite.

### The Permission Ask
> "Would it be unreasonable to ask for 15 minutes? If not, totally fine."

Weakest CTA pattern. Ask confidently and casually.

### The Evening Send
> Sent at 8:47 PM on a Wednesday

10.8% of replies came from evening sends vs 19.4% of no-replies. Afternoon wins.
