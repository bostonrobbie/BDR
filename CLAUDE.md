## Outreach SOP (for Claude)

Follow these rules exactly when writing outreach messages for Rob.

### Writing Style Rules
- **NO em dashes.** Use commas or short hyphens (-) only. Prefer commas.
- Must sound like Rob wrote it personally. Not AI-generated, not templated, not formulaic.
- Warm, conversational, low-pressure, specific, respectful.
- No feature dumping. Focus on a specific solution to something the prospect is already doing.
- Mobile-friendly: 75-95 words for Touch 1. Max 6-8 short lines with spacing.
- Clear spacing between paragraphs. No wall of text.
- **Never "show your work."** Do NOT reference LinkedIn, profiles, resume facts, or years at a company. Synthesize research into a sharp insight.
- No "I noticed" or "I saw" or "based on your profile" or resume recaps.
- No generic assertions like "you must be doing manual testing." Ask, don't assert.
- Reading level: simple words, short sentences, no corporate phrasing or buzzwords.
- At least one real question mark (preferably two: one early, one at the close).
- The close must have a question mark, tie to the value angle, and frame the meeting as a benefit to them.
- **No easy-out lines.** No "no worries," "if not, all good," "feel free to ignore." Ask confidently and casually.
- Do not frame anything as benefiting us. No "I want to show you" or "I'd love to share."

### Message Structure (C1 Style)
The C1 style synthesizes all research into a natural message that feels like one clear thought, not a formula. The 5 elements must be present but invisible as structure.

1. **Subject line** - Short (2-4 words), specific enough to open, not clickbaity.
2. **Opening question** (1-2 lines) - Curiosity-led question about a plausible initiative or challenge at their company. Do NOT cite LinkedIn. Synthesize research into an insight.
3. **Context sentence** (1-2 lines) - Why the question matters, grounded in what typically happens for teams like theirs.
4. **Proof point** (1-2 lines) - One verified customer example with real numbers, matched to vertical/pain. Mention Testsigma by name once.
5. **Close** (1 line) - Confident, casual meeting ask with question mark tied to the value angle above. No easy outs.

**What the message must NOT sound like:**
"Hey [Name], I saw you worked at [Company] for [X] years leading [team]. That must mean [assumption]. [Client] saved [number]. Would 15 minutes make sense?" That formula is dead.

### Multi-Channel Sequence
**Day 1 - Touch 1 (InMail):** Full C1 message, 70-120 words.
**Day 3 - Touch 2 (Cold Call):** 3-line call snippet (opener, pain hypothesis, bridge to ask).
**Day 5 - Touch 3 (Follow-up):** 40-70 words, new angle/proof point, softer ask.
**Day 8 - Touch 4 (Cold Call #2):** Different angle than Call #1.
**Day 10 - Touch 5 (Email):** If email available, slightly more direct.
**Day 15 - Touch 6 (Break-up):** 30-50 words, respectful close-out, never pitch.

Rules: Different proof point per touch. Touch 6 never pitches. Rob sends manually.

### Cold Call Prep
3-line cheat sheet per prospect:
1. **Opener**: "Hey [Name], this is Rob from Testsigma - [personalized hook]."
2. **Pain hypothesis**: "[Specific testing problem tied to their company]."
3. **Bridge to ask**: "We helped [proof point]. Worth 60 seconds to see if it's relevant?"

## Hard Constraints (MUST NEVER be violated)

Derived from 1,330 LinkedIn conversations (384 replies, 946 no-replies). These override intuition. Any violation = auto-rewrite.

| # | Constraint | Data Basis |
|---|-----------|------------|
| HC1 | No "reaching out" / "wanted to connect" / "I saw" / "I noticed" | -13.3 pp diff. Strongest negative signal. |
| HC2 | No role-at-company opener ("Seeing that you're the [Title] at [Company]") | -12.7 pp diff. Present in 98.8% of failures. |
| HC3 | No feature-led framing (AI, self-healing as headline) | ai_mention -8.0 pp, self_healing -6.8 pp. |
| HC4 | No messages over 120 words | Reply avg 98.7 words vs no-reply 107.7 words. |
| HC5 | No evening sends (after 6 PM) | -8.6 pp swing. |
| HC6 | No bullet-point feature lists | Dominate no-reply samples. 0 high-performers use bullets. |
| HC7 | No permission-based CTAs ("would it be unreasonable", "happy to share if helpful") | 23.5% rate, below 27.5% baseline. |

| # | Preference | Data Basis |
|---|-----------|------------|
