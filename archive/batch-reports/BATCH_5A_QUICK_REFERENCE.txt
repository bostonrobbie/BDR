MESSAGING GUIDE
===============

Key Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE):
- Never edit these messages - copy them exactly as written
- Always include "what day works" in the close
- Never send after 6 PM (best: 12-1 PM local)
- Best send days: Thursday (42.1%), Tuesday (29.6%), Friday (29.6%)
- Avoid Monday (22.9%)
- Never send the same touch twice to the same person
- Always wait 5 days between touches

What Makes These Messages Work:
1. They start with a REAL problem the prospect knows (not "I noticed")
2. They include ONE specific customer story with numbers (not feature dump)
3. They close with "what day works" tied to their situation (not generic)
4. They're exactly the right length (80-120 words for InMail, 40-70 for follow-ups)
5. They have exactly 2 question marks (not 1, not 3)

OBJECTION HANDLING
==================

If prospect says "We already have a tool":
Response: "Totally fair. A lot of teams we work with had existing tools too. The gap they kept hitting was test maintenance eating sprint capacity. Worth comparing how you're doing on that?"

If they ask "What's the price?":
Response: "Great question. Depends on your team size and what you're automating. Worth a quick call to see if it makes sense first?"

If they say "Not right now":
Response: "No problem. Things change though. What would need to happen in the next 3 months for it to be the right timing?"

All objection responses are pre-written in the batch HTML file.
