⚠️ Email is Touch 3 (Follow-Up Channel)

Email serves as the 3rd touch AFTER LinkedIn InMail Touches 1 and 2. Use email only if:
• Prospect has not replied to Touches 1 or 2 (Day 10+)
• You have a verified email address from Apollo enrichment
• You have a fresh proof point not used in the InMail sequence

Email Structure Formula (95-110 words max)

Template A: Legacy + Cloud Context
Regression across [their stack]
Hello [Name], How is your team managing test coverage as [company] balances [legacy system] and [new platform]? Keeping regression stable while shipping on both is usually where things get brittle. [Customer] was in a similar spot and cut regression from [X] to [Y] with Testsigma's AI-driven test maintenance. If [outcome] would help before [their deadline/event], what day works for a quick look at how they did it? Best, Rob
Companies migrating legacy systems to cloud or managing dual platforms (on-prem + cloud)
Template B: Org Scaling
Coverage ahead of [their growth signal]
Hello [Name], With [company] growing [specific signal], is test coverage keeping up with the release pace, or is the team spending more time on maintenance than new tests? Medibuddy automated 2,500 tests and cut maintenance in half while scaling coverage across their platform. What day works to see how they kept coverage ahead of releases without adding headcount? Best, Rob
Growing companies (funding rounds, expansion, acquisition) with scaling QA pressure
Template C: Tool Migration
[Their current tool] maintenance load
Hello [Name], How much of your team's week goes to maintaining [Selenium/Cypress/current tool] scripts vs actually improving coverage? Most teams using [tool] tell us it is about 60/40 the wrong way. [Customer] cut [tool] maintenance by 70% after switching, freeing the team to focus on releases that matter. If getting that time back before [their event] sounds useful, what day works for a quick look? Best, Rob
Companies using older tools (Selenium, Cypress, Playwright) with high maintenance burden

✅ Hard Constraints (Auto-Reject if Violated)

  • ❌ No "I noticed/saw" phrasing (-13.4pp reply rate)
  • ❌ No AI/ML/self-healing as headline feature (-9.2pp)
  • ❌ No messages over 120 words (sweet spot: 95-110 words)
  • ❌ No sends after 6 PM (12.1% vs 56.5% at lunch)
  • ❌ No bullet-point feature dumps (-2.2pp)
  • ❌ No 3+ question marks (14.3% vs 34.8% for exactly 2)
  • ❌ No em dashes. Max 1 hyphen in body.

1
Intent Detection
Source: Sales Navigator Buyer Intent signals, website analytics
Frequency: Check daily
Signals to capture:
  • Research activity on Sales Nav
  • Competitor comparisons (visiting Selenium, Playwright, etc.)
  • Tool evaluation activity
  • Job postings for QA/SDET roles
Output: List of companies/people showing intent
2
Lead Qualification
ICP Requirements:
  • QA Manager/Lead (primary)
  • Director/VP of QA (primary)
  • Software Eng Manager (secondary)
  • VP Eng/CTO (secondary, only with signals)
Company Requirements:
  • US-based (unless specified otherwise)
  • Software QA/engineering (not pharma manufacturing, biotech lab)
  • Software products or digital platforms
  • Minimum 50+ employees
3
Deduplication Check (CRITICAL)
6-Month Lookback Rules:
  • Check Apollo contacts: Has this person been contacted in last 6 months?
  • Check Apollo sequences: Is this person in ANY active sequence?
  • Check Salesforce: Is this lead owned by another BDR?
  • If assigned to another BDR → DO NOT CONTACT
  • If untouched 6+ months and not in active sequence → PROCEED
Goal: Find "hidden gems" – leads that haven't been touched in ages
4
Apollo Enrichment
Person Enrichment: Verified email, phone, current title, department
Organization Enrichment: Employee count, industry, technologies, funding, revenue
Tech Stack: Log testing tools, CI/CD, cloud providers
Budget: ~25 credits per batch of 25 prospects
5
Deep Research (3 Sources)
Source 1: LinkedIn Profile (Sales Navigator)
  • QA scope, team size signals, tech stack clues
  • Career trajectory, pain signals
  • Capture Sales Navigator profile URL
Source 2: Apollo Enrichment
  • Tech stack, company size, industry, contact data
Source 3: Company External Research
  • Product pages, engineering blogs, job postings, press releases
  • QA-relevant signals: release process, testing stack, QA org pressure
6
Message Personalization
Pre-Draft Steps:
  • Single Theme Rule: "This message is about ______"
  • QA-Relevant Research Filter: Only use research affecting QA outcomes
  • Plain Language Pass: Replace buzzwords, sentences under 15 words
  • Close Construction: Proof point outcome + prospect's version + "what day works"
Write all 4 touches:
  • Touch 1 (LinkedIn InMail, Day 1): 80-120 words, full C2 message
  • Touch 2 (LinkedIn InMail Follow-up, Day 5): 40-70 words, new angle
  • Touch 3 (Email, Day 10): 60-100 words, fresh proof point
  • Touch 4 (Call, Day 15): 30-second talk track for Rob
7
Sequence Enrollment
Apollo Sequence: "Q1 Priority Accounts"
Steps: 4 manual steps (no auto-sends)
  • Step 1: Manual LinkedIn InMail (Day 1)
  • Step 2: Manual LinkedIn InMail Follow-up (Day 5)
  • Step 3: Manual Email (Day 10)
  • Step 4: Manual Call (Day 15)
Send from: robert.gorham@testsigma.net (default email account)
8
Manual Send Execution
All steps are MANUAL:
  • Follow LinkedIn Sales Navigator Live Send SOP for InMail touches
  • Follow email formula for Touch 3 email (send via Gmail)
  • Touch 4: Rob makes the call using talk track from prep card
  • Log everything in cycle log
Important: No automated sends. Claude drafts, Rob executes.
