## WHY TOUCH 2 IS THE HIGHEST-LEVERAGE SEND IN THE SEQUENCE

Industry data consistently shows **50-70% of B2B cold email replies come on Touch 2 or later**, not Touch 1. Touch 1 plants the seed. Touch 2 converts it. With 70 contacts who received personalized Touch 1s 5-7 days ago and haven't heard back since, this is the single most impactful action available right now.

**The Pallavi lesson (our only confirmed engagement):**
Pallavi at Origami Risk replied 3 times after Touch 1, a real, substantive exchange about her Playwright test suite. The thread died when Rob asked for a demo. The pivot from "conversation" to "commitment ask" (a demo) is where most cold threads collapse. Touch 2's job is to extend the exchange, not close it.

---

## THE CORE FORMULA

Touch 2 must do three things differently from Touch 1:

1. **New angle:** Not a repeat of the T1 pitch. A question, a proof point, or a new lens on their challenge.
2. **Softer CTA:** Lower the bar. "Quick reply?" or "Happy to send a 2-min Loom" beats "15-min walkthrough?" as a second ask.
3. **Shorter body:** Touch 2 should be 60-90 words max. Shorter than T1. The brevity signals confidence and respect for their time.

## THREE TEMPLATES

### TEMPLATE A — "Question-Led" *(Default — used for most contacts)*

**Best for:** B2B SaaS, media, IoT, gaming, edtech, fleet management, telecom, any company where a diagnostic question about their testing state is natural.

**Subject line:** `One more thought, [First Name]` *(primary choice)*
**Alt subject:** `[Specific challenge] at [Company]?` *(when there's a strong specific angle)*

```
Hi [First Name],

Reaching back out on this. Still think [Company]'s situation maps well to what Testsigma does.

Quick question: [Specific diagnostic question about their current testing approach, tied to their exact context from T1].

[1-2 sentences: Why that question matters + Testsigma connection. Optional: one data point or proof reference.]

Worth a quick reply? Even a "not now" is helpful.

Cheers,
Rob
```

---

### TEMPLATE B — "Proof Point / Social Evidence" *(Regulated & compliance-heavy industries)*

**Best for:** Healthcare, life sciences, government (FedRAMP/ADA), financial services, insurance, pharmacy — orgs where data beats questions.

**Subject line:** `One more thought, [First Name]` or `[Company]: quick follow-up`

```
Hi [First Name],

One more note on this. Wanted to share something relevant.

[1-2 sentences: A proof point or result from a similar company type. Use named customers from the proof point library (Sanofi / UK Government Procurement Service / Spendflo). Tie directly to their compliance/scale context.]

Given what [Company]'s team is managing around [specific challenge from T1], I think the fit is real.

Happy to send a 2-min overview if that's easier than a call.

Cheers,
Rob
```

---

### TEMPLATE C — "Ultra-Short Confident Re-Ask" *(High-uniqueness tech orgs)*

**Best for:** Contacts at companies with very specific/unusual tech where a short, confident re-ask performs better than a long one. (Binance, Island enterprise browser, Epic Games, Interactions conversational AI, OpSec.)

**Subject line:** `One more thought, [First Name]` or `[Specific tech angle]: [Company]`

```
Hi [First Name],

Giving this one more shot.

[1 sentence referencing their specific tech context from T1, framed as a question or new angle, not a repeat].

Happy to send a quick Loom specific to [their use case] rather than jumping on a call. Might be an easier way to see if there's a fit.

Cheers,
Rob
```

## SUBJECT LINE RULES

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Never use "Re: [T1 subject]"** | False reply threading looks deceptive and damages trust |
| **Never use "Following up on my last email"** as the subject | Signals nothing new to open |
| **Primary default** | "One more thought, [First Name]" - warm, non-aggressive, works universally |
| **When to use specific subject** | When the company just had a notable event (acquisition, product launch, earnings) that you can reference - makes subject feel timely |
| **Maximum length** | 7 words or fewer |

## GRAMMAR + FORMATTING STANDARDS (March 6, 2026)
*Aligned with LinkedIn Sequence SOP and outreach_email_sop.md Phase 3*

These rules apply to every Touch 2 email draft and must be checked before any send.

**Non-negotiables:**
- **Zero em dashes (—).** None. Not one. This is the single biggest AI tell in cold email. Replace with: comma, period, colon, or parentheses.
- **Short hyphens (-) for hyphenated words only.** Compound modifiers only (e.g., follow-up, multi-tenant, cross-platform). Never use a hyphen as a sentence connector or pause.
- **No buzzwords.** Plain English only. Banned: synergy, leverage (as a verb), cutting-edge, game-changer, revolutionary, disruptive, paradigm, holistic, robust, seamless, seamlessly, turnkey, best-in-class, world-class, next-gen, bleeding edge, innovative.
- **Short sentences.** Prefer two clean sentences over one long compound sentence joined by dashes or excessive commas.
- **No walls of text.** Max 1-2 sentences per paragraph. Blank lines between blocks.
- **No emojis.** Ever.
- **No invented claims.** Only use verified proof points from the library below.

**Proof point library (named customers only):**
- Sanofi: "cut validation cycle from 3 days to under 80 minutes" / "17,000+ test executions annually" / "bugs detected 7x earlier"
- UK Government Procurement Service: "automated 4,076+ test cases, compliance documentation generated automatically"
- Spendflo: "cut testing time by 50%"

**Em dash replacement guide:**
- "Following up. Still think..." (not "Following up — still think...")
- "One more note. Wanted to share..." (not "One more note — wanted to share...")
- "...regression cycle from 3 days to 80 minutes, with full traceability..." (not "...80 minutes — with full traceability...")
- "...is the QA team using unified automation, or managing suites separately?" (not "...unified automation — or managing suites separately?")
- "...framed as a question or new angle, not a repeat" (not "...question or new angle — not a repeat")

## QA VERIFICATION PIPELINE — 10-POINT CHECKLIST

Before any Touch 2 draft is sent, it must pass all 10 checks:

- [ ] **Subject ≠ Touch 1 subject** (no "Re:" prefix, no identical subject line)
- [ ] **Opening word ≠ "Just"** (no "Just following up" or "Just checking in")
- [ ] **New information present** - at least one element not in Touch 1 (question, proof point, new angle)
- [ ] **CTA is softer than T1** - no "15-min walkthrough?" as primary ask; must be lower-friction
- [ ] **Word count ≤ 100 words** (target 65–90)
- [ ] **First name correct** — matches email_outreach_tracker.csv exactly
- [ ] **Company name correct** — no misspelling, no wrong company
- [ ] **At least one company-specific detail** — not a generic email that could go to anyone
- [ ] **Tone: peer-to-peer** — reads like a real person, not a template blast
- [ ] **Sign-off: "Cheers, Rob"** — brand consistency across all sends

## CTA VARIANTS (ROTATE TO AVOID REPETITION)

| CTA | Tone | Best used when |
|-----|------|---------------|
| "Worth a quick reply?" | Casual, low-pressure | Default |
| "Even a 'not now' is helpful." | Transparent, disarming | When trying to get any signal |
| "Happy to send a 2-min Loom if that's easier than a call." | Softer commitment bar | Second-touch re-ask |
| "Even just curious: how are you currently handling [X]?" | Invites a reply without commitment | When question-led approach |
| "Totally fine if timing's off — but worth a reply if you're still exploring." | Empathetic close | Longer-in-pipe contacts |
